Features

Background

A market is used to exchange or trade goods or services by buyers and sellers. A capital market is a market which is used to trade financial securities. Examples for these financial securities would be bonds, stocks, and foreign exchange.

Traders buy, keep and sell these securities with the motive of making profit in short term or long term. In order to make profits, traders from different backgrounds follow different methods. This can range from randomly buying and selling securities to the acquisition of theoretical knowledge and the use of highly sophisticated systems to facilitate trading decisions. Most of the time, the profit or loss they make depends on the approach they take in the market. It is important to arm the traders with the proper domain knowledge in order to make them profit makers. If the proper knowledge is not delivered to the traders, it will refrain them from investing in the capital market.

Further skeptical opinions that the general public have on the capital market as a possible mean of investment destination has made the prospective investors refrain from investing in the capital market.

A country needs active, risk seeking investors in the capital market for the economic growth of the country. There are organizations and institutions in every country which are focusing on providing the interested parties with the necessary domain knowledge. The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is one such organization in Sri Lanka. In their process of educating the general public about the capital market, they have identified the need of capital market games in order to allow any potential investor to get hands on experience, as to how investing in a capital market happens.

Problem Identified

The existing capital market gaming solutions lack the necessary extensibility that allows a 3rd party to extend those existing solutions and make use of that existing infrastructure to create new games. Since most of them are closed source products, even a capable developer cannot leverage on those solutions source code in order to add that missing functionality to those existing solutions.

Our Approach

Provide the basic building blocks for developing capital market games as a customizable framework so that the game developer can focus on developing game aspects and use the framework components to model market environment.

Project Objectives

Main objective of this project is to provide a platform;

      to increase awareness about the capital market investing among prospective investors

      for veteran/ intermediate investors to test their market strategies/ gain experience

      for students and researchers to gain knowledge in capital market investing

      for a company/ organization to test their business decisions on a simulated customized environment

by developing a highly customizable capital market gaming framework which can be used to create various capital market gaming scenarios up on need and gives the best possible real world capital market experience.

Product Features

The main features of the product would include;

      Create games with multiple models Create games which can give user experience on different aspects of the market with different difficulty levels.

      Plug-in different data sets Ability to import data from different stock markets.

      Two layer customizability Programmatic level for introducing novel game models and user interface with support for customizing current game models.

      Centralized authentication LDAP based centralized authentication

      Intelligent artificial players Artificial agents for simulating multi-user environment

      Market reports Modules for obtaining reports on market status of current game

      Analysis tools Integrated financial analysis tools to analyze market reports to help users to make informed investing decisions.

Scope

The investovator framework is proposed as a full platform for developing capital market based (simulation) games with support for extension and supporting users of several levels (developers and end users). Given the time constraints of the (CS-4202) module we had to limit our scope of implementation to several aspects after referring literature on current developments, available frameworks and analyzing the amount of work to be carried out.

The scope of the whole project falls under two major steps; development of the backend framework and development of web based UI framework. Backend framework provides support for trading, simulations and data retrieval. This is mainly focused for developers. Web based UI framework helps an end user to graphically design customizable games. To manage the workload we have divided work to some phases.

      Phase 1 Completion of core functions of the backend framework

      Phase 2 - Development of some demo games on top of the developed framework

      Phase 3 Development of UI framework

The trading system supported is limited to standard double auction system used by Colombo Stock Exchange (CSE) and will not support customizable rules. Extending trading rules of framework for other stock exchanges will need developer intervention to develop modules for different rule sets.

The gaming framework will be developed with support for different datasets from different stock markets using generalized data retrieval interface, however data converter component used for converting each dataset to generic format will be only provided for data standard used by CSE. In order to use with data with different standards, converter components will have to be developed separately.
